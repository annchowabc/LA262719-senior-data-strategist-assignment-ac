Executive Summary – Privacy & Ethics Considerations for Go‑Live
The rollout of auto‑labelling and Copilot assistance will materially strengthen the Assembly’s information governance and improve staff productivity. It also introduces significant privacy and ethics risks that must be actively managed. The most important issues for go‑live fall into three categories: exposure risk, misuse risk, and trust risk.

1. Exposure Risk:  
Copilot increases the discoverability of sensitive information. Even when access is technically authorized, AI summarization can unintentionally surface personal data, member casework, legal opinions, audit findings, and security documentation. These domains carry the highest harm potential under the Information Protection deck.
Action: Restrict Copilot to zero‑retention mode and limit access to Confidential and Restricted content to explicitly authorized users.

2. Misuse Risk:  
AI systems can misclassify content, hallucinate details, or generate summaries that appear authoritative but are incomplete or misleading. This is especially risky for legal, audit, financial, and security content, where professional judgment is required.
Action: Require human review for all Confidential and Restricted labels during the first 90 days and mandate source‑anchored outputs for all non‑Public summaries.

3. Trust Risk:  
The Assembly’s obligations to democratic accountability mean that AI‑generated content must not obscure authorship or reduce transparency. Copilot could unintentionally create inequities across departments if some groups benefit more from AI assistance than others.
Action: Require human attribution for all public‑facing content and monitor usage patterns to ensure equitable access.

Domains requiring special caution:
Member Support: Highly personal and politically sensitive; Copilot access should be deferred pending a dedicated privacy assessment.
Legal Opinions: Privileged and easily misinterpreted; summarization should be disabled.
Audit Findings: Contain operational weaknesses; Copilot access should be restricted entirely.
Closed Proceedings: Privileged deliberations; Copilot should not be enabled.

Requests in the brief that should be handled differently:
Do not enable Copilot for Member Support, Legal, Audit, or Closed Proceedings at go‑live.
Do not auto‑label legacy records without manual review.
Do not allow Copilot to generate detailed summaries of Restricted content.
Do not treat Confidential content as safe for general summarization.
